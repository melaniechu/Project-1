EAST meets WEST

This is Mel. She is a rising senior at Northeastern University, majoring in marketing and interactive media. She grew up in Monroe Township, New Jersey with her parents and two older sisters, Justine and Lindsay. Mel's interests include dancing and cooking. After graduation she wants to explore the world and understand international behaviors in the marketplace.

This is Jen. She is also a fourth-year senior at Northeastern University, majoring in behavioral neuroscience. Jen was born and raised in San Diego, California with her parents and younger sister, Julianna. Her hobbies include boxing and dancing. After she graduates she hopes to work for Teach for America and later conduct research to help find a cure for cancer.

Mel and Jen met their freshmen year when they both danced on Kinematix Dance Troupe at Northeastern. The two were also in similar clubs and after spending practically every day together through eboard meetings, they became best friends. Since California was a far distance away from Boston, Jen began staying at Mel's house in New Jersey over Thanksgiving Break. This past summer she decided to return the favor and hosted Mel in San Diego at the end of summer break. THe two got to explore where the other calls home and get to know each other even better.

EAST New Jersey
Comfi
When you see either of us, I wouldn't be surprised if we're eating. Both of us love food and therefore I had to take Jen to one of my family's favorite brunch spots, Comfi. The cozy family-owned restaurant serves authentic Polish cuisine alongside breakfast classics in heaping portions. You'll always leave with a happy stomach and a box of leftovers to bring home.
Sculpture Grounds
One of my favorite places to drive to when it's nice out is the Grounds for Sculpture in Hamilton. The 42-acre open space is full of contemporary sculpture and figures for you to discover, admire, and take the occasional silly photo with. We visited during an exhibition of Seward Johnson's most famous works, including Daydream which was recreated about 40 feet tall.
Princeton
Another nearby town that attracts a lot of visitors is Princeton. The university is made up of beautiful, historic buildings surrounding by small cafes and shops. Despite the colder weather we made sure to taste ice cream from every spot we could find. The shops were also perfect to find unique gifts right before the holiday season.
WEST California
Disneyland
I got to explore Disneyland and California Adventure for the first time with Jen and her younger cousins. It was truly magical as a I got over my fear of roller coasters (somewhat), met some of my favorite characters, and ended the night with the Electrical Parade.
Getty Museum
Knowing my love of art, we visited the Getty Museum during our day trip to Los Angeles. The expansive museum is home to a range of art from various artists, styles, and time periods. We admired the exhibits, the beautiful botanical gardens, and the showcased exhibit on ancient Chinese caves.
La Jolla
I wanted to see what made California beaches so special and La Jolla did not disappoint. The sand was soft, the water was clear, and best of all, you had the chance to get up close and personal with the seals lounging on the shore. With acai bowls in hand, we relaxed and watched them flop around all afternoon.
